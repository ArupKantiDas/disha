# Disha 60-second demo script

A tight, repeatable run. Have the API and web running, or the deployed URLs open on a phone.

## Setup (before you start the clock)
- Open Disha on a phone-width screen.
- Have the fake booking screenshot handy if you want to show Door C.

## The 60 seconds

**0:00 The hook (say while the empty state is on screen)**
> "Every carbon app is a guilt machine you open once. It tells you what you already burned. Disha works *before* you spend, and it makes logging a single sentence."

**0:08 Type a decision**
- Type: *"Kolkata to Bangalore for a 3-day work trip"* → Compare.
- (Or tap the ✈️ seed chip.)

**0:14 The card lands (~3s)**
> "Gemini read that sentence into real options. But it did *not* invent the carbon. A verified engine did, from CEA and rail factors."
- Point at the three columns: **carbon, cost, time** on every option.
- Train is the green pick: ~24 kg vs the flight's ~460 kg.

**0:28 The nudge line**
> "Train saves about 436 kg CO₂, it's cheaper, and you can work the whole way. The greener choice is also the self-interested one that's why people actually take it."

**0:36 The honesty beat (tap the 🚗 EV vs petrol chip)**
> "And we're honest about India. An EV here saves less than the marketing claims, because the grid is coal-heavy about 0.72 kg per kWh. Look: the EV only saves ~17 kg, not the 'zero emissions' you've been sold."

**0:46 Commit + the counter**
- Tap **"I'll take this"** on the greener option.
> "One tap. The lifetime 'carbon avoided' counter ticks up. Reduction framed as a growing win, not a shrinking guilt budget."

**0:54 The screenshot door (optional, if time)**
> "And you don't even have to type. Share a screenshot of the flight you were about to book Disha reads the route and price, treats that as your default, and shows the greener thing you didn't see."

**0:58 Close**
> "We made logging a sentence, and replaced the guilt number with one action you can take before you spend a rupee."

## The one line for judges
> "The model maps your decision to verified factors. It does not guess the carbon. That's the whole credibility of the product, and it's enforced in the code."
